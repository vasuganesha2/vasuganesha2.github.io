Chapter 01

Combinatorics

Counting, arrangements, and the art of intelligent enumeration

permutations combinations inclusion-exclusion pigeonhole generating functions
01 Basic Counting Formulas ▾
Combination (choose r from n)
\[ \binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \]
Also written C(n,r) or nCr. Number of ways to choose r items from n without regard to order.
Permutation (arrange r from n)
\[ P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} \]
Ordered arrangements. P(n,n) = n!
Stars and Bars
\[ \binom{n+k-1}{k-1} \]
Ways to distribute n identical items into k distinct bins (non-negative integers).
02 Inclusion-Exclusion Principle ▾
Two sets
\[ |A \cup B| = |A| + |B| - |A \cap B| \]
Three sets
\[ |A \cup B \cup C| = |A|+|B|+|C| - |A\cap B| - |B\cap C| - |A\cap C| + |A\cap B\cap C| \]
General form (n sets)
\[ \left|\bigcup_{i=1}^n A_i\right| = \sum_{\emptyset \neq S \subseteq [n]} (-1)^{|S|+1} \left|\bigcap_{i \in S} A_i\right| \]
Alternating signs: add singletons, subtract pairs, add triples, …
At least k sets
\[ \sum_{j=k}^{n} (-1)^{j-k} \binom{j-1}{k-1} \sum_{|I|=j} \left|\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i\right| \]
Counts elements belonging to at least k of the sets.
03 Binomial Identities ▾
Binomial Theorem
\[ (x+y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^k y^{n-k} \]
Pascal's Identity
\[ \binom{n}{k} = \binom{n-1}{k-1} + \binom{n-1}{k} \]
Vandermonde's Identity
\[ \binom{m+n}{r} = \sum_{k=0}^{r} \binom{m}{k}\binom{n}{r-k} \]
Sum of a row
\[ \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} = 2^n \]
04 Derangements & Pigeonhole ▾
Derangements D(n)
\[ D(n) = n! \sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{(-1)^k}{k!} \approx \frac{n!}{e} \]
Permutations where no element appears in its original position. Recurrence: D(n) = (n−1)(D(n−1) + D(n−2)).
Pigeonhole Principle
\[ n > mk \implies \exists \text{ bin with} > k \text{ items} \]
If n items go into m bins and n > mk, then at least one bin contains more than k items.
